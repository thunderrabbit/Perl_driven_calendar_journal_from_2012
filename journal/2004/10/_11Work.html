1:04pm JST Monday 11 October 2004

For the first two races, we couldn't get the software to work, so OM decided for the next race we could just copy the bets placed in the other room.

We went over, each using the printers as tables and hand copied all the bets, reducing the value to 10% of that which was being bet on those cards.

Okay finished. 7 betting cards. Ready, set, went down to the windows five minutes later than usual. Ian was running to get there on time. I walked fast; I knew we would have time, and if not, that Ian would understand. Gave the cards to the woman, and one of them beeped. One of the cards I had written didn't have the bet amount marked. Then three of them beeped. The cards that Ian had bet didn't have the track name, race number, or bet type listed.

I was practically laughing; this obviously absolutely was not going to work.

For a bit of added drama, when Ian's guy called him, he (the guy) said the wrong bet amount, making Ian think that he must have left a card in the chaos. That turned out to be the guy's fault, but it confirmed for Ian that this was not going to be attempted again.
