1:54pm JST Friday 1 October 2004

Rode TJ Bike to my Japanese lesson in Shibuya. 46 minutes. Before the lesson, I bought my customary Vanilla Cream Frappaciono, and realized that I needed some food; I hadn't eaten yet. I was like, "行ってきます。" to the girls who giggled and "行ってらしゃい" and then more giggling for "只今" and "おかいりなさい。" These are greetings normally used at home; I was implying that the Starbucks is like home to me. Japanese humor. I think.

During the lesson, Ayana had a phone call with her boss and was pretty upset with the amount of work they've had to do (working until 7am the next day many days per week, causing her to cancel our previous two lessons) but it was all good; I wrote a bit of a narrative for my bicycle adventure that day.

家をでて東急線のわきをちょっと行って、それから、右に曲（ま）がって線の 下を通って大きいみちを左に曲がる。ちょっといつも次のしんごうは　あか、 でも今日あお。だから、車と一緒に行ったなぜならいぱい人が歩道（ほどう）に いたからです。むさしこすぎ駅と新幹線（しんかんせん）の間の橋（はし）で なんぶ線の上を渡る（わたる）。その歩道はグラスがある。だから、気をつけた。 次のしんごうはあおと思います。だから、車のとなりで　みちの　橋行った、 でも次のしんごうは　あか。だから、ちょっと待った。
