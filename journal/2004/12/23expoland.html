7:44pm JST Thursday 23 December 2004

Big fun at Expoland. It's got one nice 60 second suspended coaster called Orochi that we rode three or four times. I got a video from the front seat. It's not online yet.

They have a long coaster that pretends to be a racer, but is actually one single long track that crosses one time; so it's like we ride both sides of the racer in one turn.

It's called Daidarasaurus, and according to rcdb, it used to be a racer, but was converted to one long coaster.

They've got a coaster that's in the running for two prizes

  1. Most inversions per unit length in a circuit coaster: turn, chain-lift, turn, loop, turn, double-corkscrew, turn, station.

  2. Silliest name: Space Salamander

Only Space Salamander allowed me to ride while wearing my glasses.

We rode it 2 or 3 times.

I have the most pictures of it because it's right below the giant ferris wheel in the park.

They have a standing coaster that looks like it must be an original; it had basically no sides, and the weirdest, most difficult to get into harness thing. Instead of an over-shoulders harness, it has two butterfly type things through which we put our arms, and then fold-down bar behind which we have to stand, and then a bicycle seat to sit on. It took me a while to understand what they were trying to get me to do when I was getting on the ride.

It was dark when we rode it, and I wasn't allowed to wear my glasses, and it was really cold, so... it wasn't all that great.
