I slept and dreamt after the exercises.

In a school type situation, where I was new to the area, but it turns out it was a prison. I was in a wrong area with some others, out in the rocky vallied terrain where some trains would occassionally rush. The people who had been in the prison longer were more comfortable with the trains, not nervous about being squashed or seen like I was.

I hypothosize now that maybe they didn't mind dying; the prison may have been boring; they could use the train to get out permanently.

I was on top of a train with a woman, kinda holding her, and I said, "things are about to change around here; I came with some really good people who will make a difference," she seemed happy to hear that, and, possibly, believed me.

The trains drove into a building with small French doors for the trains to go through. The trains were small, so we had to duck to get into the building, where there were lots of ladders and things to climb, and a general store (closed) and post office window (also closed). We were there at night and on of my new friends was standing right on the track as I was climbing up a ladder, and I could see a train was coming. I told my friend, but they didn't move, so I was like, "so get off the track!" and they moved, but not like, [oh man, thanks for telling me] but just moved as the train came. This train was smaller than the freight train that had come through; it was no wider than the track. The train saw my friend standing there, and with a growly face put its arms out and tried to stop itself from going out of the room, bracing against the doorframe, but its momentum was too great, and my friend was like, "bah" and kicked at the train's arms and it flew on through the door. Apparently being seen by a train (of that size/shape/character) wasn't important. I wondered why they weren't concerned about the train reporting the sighting; maybe it would forget as soon as it left the area.
