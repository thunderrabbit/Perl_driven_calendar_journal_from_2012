12:01pm JST Wednesday 18 February 2004

Some time ago (10 years?) I read most of Dale Carnegie's How to Win Friends and Influence People. Here's a cool thing about Gaba; they gave us each a copy of the book on our second day of training. The title of this book I thought weird, but the book is money.

Today I read chapter one on the train, and I had a vision in my mind of giving a copy to Steve at Nova who more often criticizes than praises. Then I read the quote attributed to Ben Franklin "I will speak ill of no man ... and speak all the good I know of everybody."

Four stars. Plus a 2x4 to my brain: I have spoken a lot of ill of Steve. I acknowledge that it may not have been in the best interest of all involved.

Another four stars on the facing page: "To show you I'm sure that you'll never do this again, I want you to service my F-51 tomorrow."

Thanks, Dale.

On the train home tonight, a man who spoke pretty good if accented English asked me where I had purchased my copy of How to Win Friends and Influence People. He was reading a Japanese version of a different Dale Carnegie book, the name of which I seem to have forgotten.

I gave him a map to Gaba's office as a place he could find out where they bought the books.
