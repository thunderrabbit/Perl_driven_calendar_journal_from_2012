I've just been snoozing / meditating / dreaming while listening to remix.nin.com.

I finally decided I should sleep, so I looked for a way to turn the device OFF. I found what I thought was the switch and clicked it, and the animations stopped, but the music kept playing. Then I realized the device I had ws actually two units connected by a simple sliding together interface. One was the portable music player (actually about the size of a CD Walkman, and the other was the connection to the internet. Holy moly! That is so cool!! I looked at how the inteface worked; as I pressed the units together, two phone wires would extend out from the player side and connect with the internet connection side of the device. The inteface was actually quite complex to do all that, but worked flawlessly each time.

I realized a bit later that in a different reality, I was still able to hear the music because I was listening to it on "headphones." They connect with a simple connector (not as cool, but equally flawless in operation) to my internet side, a "laptop." The only bad thing about this system is that the headphones don't download the music. I call dibs on this idea!!! I'll email it to myself and see if that works as a patent protection.

But that's not why I'm writing. remix.nin.com is really sweet. I think this kind of mass collaboration has a definite place in the evolvernet. I call dibs on this word!!!
