Ami headed home ahead of me to P, and I joined her in my room with ice cream. She was laying on the bed and I straddled over her and ate ice cream, feeding her small amounts when she opened her mouth. I tried to let some ice cream slide off the end of the spoon into her mouth, but it was frozen to the spoon and didn't move.

Tried it again with some meltier ice cream and she said, "ちゃんと下さい" which I could translate as "carefully, please," or "be exact," but I missed and it ended up on her neck.

I said, "sorry" and ate the ice cream and helped clean her neck. She wiped it cleaner with her shirt and refused to eat any more.

I climbed off her and asked if she was angry at me.

"Yes."

"Why?"

"I said ちゃんと下さい。"

"I said I was sorry."
