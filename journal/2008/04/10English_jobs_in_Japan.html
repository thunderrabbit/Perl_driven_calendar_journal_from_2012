My current, official quick answer for people interested in teaching / working in Japan.

I don't remember if you've visited Japan before, but if not, and if you have a few thousand dollars,
you may benefit just be coming to see how you like it and look for a job while you're a tourist.  I
imagine you'd certainly find work if you put effort into looking.

Be sure to check 

http://www.gaijinpot.com/

Teaching through the JET program will get you involved with a tightly knit group that teaches in
high schools around Japan.  

http://www.jetprogramme.org/

If you teach through JET, you'll prolly work in a small city, far from
the hubs like Tokyo, Osaka, Kyoto, etc.  Downsides: super long preparation time (like 9 months), and
they only import teachers once per year.  I know some peeps who've done this, so you can talk to
them if you're interested.
