Two journal entries from Colleen Crotty

1:01 pm Monday, December 30th, 2002 - onions like apples

last night rob and i were sitting in the same room, talking on AIM,
having by far one of the best conversations in the history of
conversations, combining the real world with our internet conversation
and though it *sounds* really lame, it was the coolest thing ever if
you'd been there.

then rob paid me $5 to take a bite of an onion like it was an
apple. and i did it. and then i paid him $5 to do it too. so in the
end, we both wound up with the same amount of money that we started
with, and onion breath. i love rob soooooo sososososososo much. i was
all suuper down about stuff and he saved the night. 

Suga Daddy.

2:20 am Tuesday, December 31st, 2002 - 
over the laws of light, over the moon by midnight

it hurt my heart reading his journal. was i just like that to him? a
good story to tell friends? a fun adventure. i thought it was more
than that, but i was wrong about so many things when it came to that
person. it hurt that it was written somewhere i could obviously see
it, although i'm sure i was the farthest thing from his mind when he
wrote it. made what we'd shared seem, less sincere, somehow. i have
cried more since i got to dallas than i have for a while. first she's
gone and not coming, and my heart ached, then i read his journal, and
my heart bled. 

thank god for rob. that's all i can say. and it's not even anywhere
near enough to just thank "god" for him. we talked for a long time
tonight, and this time for real, not a silly aim thing. we talked for
a long time. i shared things with him i have shared with few
others. things that hurt and made me cry again. i hate crying. at
first it seemed comforting, but as the years of incessant crying
continued, it only makes me feel weak to cry.

i was shocked how much i felt hurt and small reading that entry. it
felt like everything that had hurt me before was happening all over
again, but the more i think about it, the more i feel like i have no
right to feel hurt by that journal. aren't i the one that stopped
things?  yes. did i have good reason to? yes. did i like having to say
that i didnt want to speak to you? not one bit, because that would
mean i would have one more branch on the tree of things i have to look
back on sadly. one more thing thta has hurt me in my life.

i dont think anybody should experience so much sadness, anger, hurt,
loneliness, and bitterness before they are even 19 yrs old.
