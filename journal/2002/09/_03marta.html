9:10am CDT Tuesday 3 September 2002

Last night I'm guessing around 5am, Marta woke me up and was all sad and crying that she hasn't slept much in the last four days. This is the first time I've learned of this problem that is only just now freaking her out because she has a full schedule planned today: school, take her dad to the airport, see Chucky's brown belt test tonight.

I listened and gave her a suggestion of counting my heartbeats and that calmed her down and then I suggested counting her breaths and that put her to sleep for a while.

She woke up still panicked this morning, and has called someone and will skip her first class today. She's still going to try to attend her second one.

This panickiness is all due to her not bleeding a big flood that she has expected after a miscarriage. I've been visualizing a flush of energy through her this morning to help facilitate that.

OH! I forgot to rewrite an entry that was deleted before the Tulsa Rally. On Friday I accompanied Marta to the clinic at her school and she learned that either she was pregnant and had complications or she had already miscarried.

And this is when she's expecting to bleed a lot to flush this miscarriage out, but hasn't really gotten that flood yet. A little spotting, but nothing major.
