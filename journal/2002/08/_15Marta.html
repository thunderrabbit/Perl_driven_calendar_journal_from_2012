10:11am CDT Thursday 15 August 2002

Marta is in the shower crying right now. It seems to be a combination of factors, but she won't talk to me about it:

  • The fact that I'm leaving her today for the Bibby's
    I told her a few days ago "I'm leaving on Thursday" and she apparently didn't hear me, and yesterday she was all surprised and shit when I mentioned it. She got all quiet, which I noticed, but didn't notice certainly enough to call her on it or make an assumption that she got quiet because I was leaving.
    Later, when I asked about her mood (she seemed really sad) she said she didn't want to talk about it with me. So we sat in silence a bit until she said, "I have abandonment issues; it's not your fault; it's my stuff.."
  • She's reading a book of plays/short stories by female black authors
    I'm sure these stories are not all fun and games and shit
  • Her friends have been fighting each other a lot - Cris and Laura were apparently screaming at each other, each trying to simultaneously break up and keep the other one close (from what I can tell), and Marta thinks of herself as Mama Marta, and all her friends come to her, but she has no where to turn for support. Except to cry in the shower.

    • When she told me she was having abandonment issues I was all, "eep" in my mind, and turned off any concept of any more sex with her. She hasn't approached me for sex as she had in the past few days, and I certainly didn't thwart her advances, but now I would. Really.

    She seems to do better when she writes. I think she's working on an assignment for Natalie, her mentor person who (*) has been helping her with writing plays and such. They really are impressed with one another (Marta and Natalie) and glad to be working together.

    I noted that she seemed better after writing this way: after we went to the museum and came back, she was all quiet (during that drive home is when she brought up abandonment issues) and we had scheduled to go get ice cream with Erin Dunn (a youth with many many issues of her own (parents blame her for shit (being born) and call her a bad kid) that she brought out a lot at SWUUSI) so I figured it would be nice if Marta was not all emotional, and I was just about to confront her and have her get that shit out when I came out and saw she was working happily on her writing, instead of moping around the house. Fair enough; I let it go.
