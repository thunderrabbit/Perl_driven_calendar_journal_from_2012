Chez Soness features a porcelain waste disposal unit designed for a traditional asian squat. To support booties used to sitting, a plastic seat converts the squatter into an actual throne, if one has a good enough imagination. People with really good imaginations might even believe that the previous seater's seat wasn't broken. However, even a cursory inspection of the bottom of the old seat would reveal I had implemented a fix of plastic bottle caps and chopsticks to support the seat and keep its parts together, respectively. This lasted a few months before its own failure.

Today, I rode Soness's yellow bike to Shimachu, and purchased a new throne to augment the porcelain squatter. I chose the one whose plastic seemed sturdier (thicker and more flexible, actually) than a new 5800 yen unit identical(?) to the broken one at the crib. 11,500 yen for our lovely new chair, but get this: it has a lock type thing to attach it to the existing porcelain, and it has some kind of hydraulics that ensure it closes silently, so no one can crash it.
