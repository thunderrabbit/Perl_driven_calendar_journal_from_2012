Mice and Men, a play based on the book by Steinbeck, was performed this weekend in Ebisu by TIP. I wanted to see the tidal wave more than I wanted to see the show, so I felt a bit lucky when the trains were stopped for the tidal wave. Unfortunately, there was no tidal wave, but the trains didn't restart after I deemed things "all clear." I guess my sway in Japan isn't as pervasive as I might have hoped.

Anyway, I got to the theater just as the play was over, and met Emiko, crying, on her way out; we were supposed to have attended together, so I figured at least I could show up. We ate a bit, though it took her a bit to regain her appetite after the play. We went to karaoke so she could cry in relative privacy.

In that relative privacy, I gave her the big news that I can't kiss on her anymore and still be in integrity with my mission. I didn't say it quite like that; I said basically if I kiss you, and fuck you, I will throw you away. I'd rather respect you, love you, and be your friend. She cried, saying she would miss her Daddy, even though he wasn't real. I was like, "I can still be your Daddy; I just can't kiss you." She goes, "it's not the same," so I respected that.

But I suspected it could be beneficial even without kisses, so I said, "even if it's not real, Daddy still loves you," and she began to cry and I sat her in my lap and held her and held her. To support her, I asked if she wanted to be "Daddy's girl," "Daddy's little girl," or "Daddy's big girl," and she said she wanted to be "Daddy's little girl; I want to be a baby," so I hugged her and called her baby and baby girl and all the loving things I could do. She cried and cried, and I took it one step further, "I'm sorry I was gone for so long, but now I'm back, and I will never leave you," and she cried more and hugged more, so I repeated that a few times during our two hour karaoke adventure.

At one point, because she's still got sex and love confused (as do I), she kissed me fully tonguely and I responded with equally tonguey kisses. (*) I knew that this was a goodbye kiss, so I believe it more therapeutic than damaging.

(*) I must admit here that I got a bit lost in the action and didn't immediately notice when she began to ease up on the kisses. It took me about a second or two to also temper my tongue.

Near the end, she was feeling better, and was like, "so, can I tell you about the play?" and I was like, "of course!!" and she told me about the play until we were interrupted by time to leave.

Afterward, we took the yamanote line from Ebisu to Tokyo, on which we were able to sit after Shinagawa, and I cuddled her some more as she cried. Continuing on from "I'll never leave you," in karaoke room, I said on the train, "I'm glad we're back together." She was like, "I don't know what you are talking about," and I go, "you don't remember because you were just a baby, and she goes, "do *you* remember?" and I was like, "yeah!?" while shaking and nodding my head teasingly. She cried some more as I held her.

In Tokyo station, where I got the 22:13 train to head home, Emiko sat with me for a bit on the train before it left. We were in the front of the green car, in the seats at platform level, giving some hugs and platonic kisses just until a minute or two before the train left.

After we were clear of the station, I moved up to the upper deck, cause that's where I really wanted to sit.
