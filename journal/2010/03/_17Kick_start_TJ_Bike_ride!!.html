Hello Kickstarters!!

I'm Rob Nugen (thunderrabbit on the site), currently living in Japan with a bicycle tour itching to begin. In 2006, I rode TJ Bike (my trusty bicycle) for two months around Japan. (http://robnugen.com/travel/tjbike/kyoto2006/) With knowledge gained from that experience, I'd like to do another trip around Japan, but with better tools and networking ability.

The super-most optimal would be live streaming video of the ride (similar to http://tamegoeswild.com/tm/) though there may not be sufficient cellular coverage in all areas I visit in Japan.

Even if the video part can't fully happen, I'll definitely be photo-blogging my ride on a daily / hourly basis (twitpic / facebook / picasa / etc). And I'll be journaling the whole time (I have been journaling online since before "blog" was a word haha http://robnugen.com/cgi-bin/journal.pl) so at the end of the ride, I intend to digitally publish a book of the ride (a la cafepress or others (still need to do some research on this)).

Riding along with me will be a cute stuffed animal (probably a tiger (or something else, but definitely cute!)) who will appear in many of the photographs I take along the trip (similar to my rabbit Francois (e.g. http://robnugen.com/images/peeps/francois/2007/with_jason/)). The tiger will get his/her own twitter/twitpic account on which tiger-featured photos will be published.

The word will be spread via
http://www.facebook.com/thunderrabbit
http://twitter.com/thunderrabbit
http://www.couchsurfing.org/
http://thepinkcow.com/NewHome_e.htm (I hang out there a lot and will do an fundraiser there)
http://halffastcycling.com/
Soness, my housemate, who has a *lot* of connections! http://www.soness.com/

Rewards include:
* community vote on the name of the tiger!
* people get to take an armchair bicycle ride around Japan without sweating!
* postcards sent to top 10 highest backers
* hardcopies of the book to backers above a certain price not yet calculated $25? $30?
* one lucky bidder gets to keep the tiger (and password to the twitter account)

I'm open to ideas on this.

upfront expenses:
* bicycle powered phone charger (does this exist?)
* bicycle tune up
* small video camera and uplink ability (I need to do more research on this)

ongoing expenses:
* food, bicycle maintenance (lodging will be outside or with people via couchsurfing.org)
* occasional shower + bath in public baths

afterward expenses:
* time to put together the book

I'm hoping to start in May 2010, and be back in Tokyo before weather gets too cold. The ride will almost certainly happen even without kickstarter.com money, but it sure would be nice to form a support network this way. :-)

Thanks!
- Rob
