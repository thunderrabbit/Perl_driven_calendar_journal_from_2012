Last I saw her, she was saying HI to Tomoko after I lost my keys at Tokyo Dome City.

Today I mentioned that I hadn't seen her in a while (except for that) and she was like, "?" and then remembered. (For the briefest of moments (not like nano and pico seconds, but maybe 1.5 seconds) I thought I was mistaken) She was like, "oh, do you like Tomoko?"

"Yah; she's nice, but I have a girlfriend."

"Japanese?"

"No, she's in the US."

"Mm"

"So what's up?"

"mmmmm. I have love problems."

And we spent the lesson talking about her relationship concerns using the target languages "I (don't) like" and answers to "how often..." and "how much..."

I'm glad to know a bit more about her than nothing.

Of course, I'm talking about Keiko (get it, work cake) whose smile is lovely. She's 22 and in this long distance relationship with a Japanese cat with whom she fell in love at first sight in the US 2.5 years ago. They've been in a long distance relationship ever since. I was like, "how much do you like this cat?" etc and eventually brought her around to the question, "is it worth it?"

"no way"

She said it immediately; no hesitation. I wrote it down and put a square around it for emphasis. "There's your answer. You said it yourself. You're not ready to settle down."
