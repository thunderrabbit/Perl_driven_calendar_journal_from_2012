I finished up at work then called her as soon as I got outside. She wasn't too far away.

- - - -

I had a totally lovely time with her. We ate at an okonomiyaki restaurant; I was polite as possible; we served each other (she showed the way) and chatted lots. She asked most of the questions; she's had practice at asking questions of teachers, so I didn't feel too bad that I wasn't quite as well versed at querying. I was mostly preoccupied with you are so fucking CUTE!!!, and trying to temper that with memories of disasters when I followed that mindset.

I showed her the Japanese I had written recently, including the kanji for shinkansen. She corrected one hiragana (the rest had been corrected by Ayana) and I gave her some English grammar on 1/2 a piece of paper that included a single "shinkansen" written on the top. That paper (including the date (I wrote the 14th at first) using numerals and kanji) was my gift to her for this evening.

We walked around Cinecitta, and set a date to see Kill Bill on Thursday. I verified three times that though there is a lot of blood, she still wants to see the movie. Yes, yes, yes.

- - - -

Walked in the new building across from the movie place to see what's up in there and were yammering a bit when she told me that the UFO catchers had live lobsters in them a couple years ago.

"Miki." I said matter of factly like, you know it's not polite to lie.

But she assured me it was true. Well, I have seen fighting betas in UFO catchers; and in fact, that was only a few days ago (well, a week now) right downstairs at the game center. Kimashyou.

So we went down and ended up with some cute pictures from the photo booth thing. She put a heart on it and then erased it, and then I put it back on there, and she put several more hearts on it. My god she is cute.

I put a small copy of the picture on my computer, underneath where my right hand will cover it if I'm typing and anyone glances, they will not see it. Choto dangerous, cause I sometimes write at work, but I'll just have to be careful. Or get a new job.

Choto dangerous, I'm partially afraid, cause janette will definitely see it. And though nothing has happened, and I haven't even actually fantasized about fucking Miki, I'm definitely smitten with her.

But I don't know really anything about her still. This was only our first hang out time.

(There were no fighting betas in a UFO catcher, but we walked around the entire area, marveling at how people could be so smitten by the games.)

- - - -

It was 10pm by then, and I kinda needed to get back in case janette was home to receive my phone call (though I was pretty sure she wasn't) and we parted.

Well, we stood for a long time in Kawasaki station, as she gave me my homework for the week: the names of the dates of the month. There is an easy to remember pattern for about 1/2 of the days, but the other 1/2 are less patternful.

Finally, finally, we parted. No hug. But lots of waves.

Oh, Miki, what a pity, you don't understand. You take me by the heart when you take me by the hand. *
