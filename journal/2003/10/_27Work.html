Rocked into voice and talked about rollercoasters with a cat named Takahiro, a woman named Kaori (not the cop), and a common voice student whose name I tried desperately to remember, but still haven't yet. I remembered Kaori's name, and hadn't met the guy yet. The other woman came in late and missed the name recognition festivities.

Miho and Makoto are level 4s and were my second lesson. I brought the National Geographic magazine page about glacier exploration (February 1996) and opened to the page with a guy (Stefan Geissler?) hanging from the ceiling on the top left picture and another cat hanging from a huge stalactite icicle on the right page.

To teach this as a lesson to high level 4 students who have done everything else, before showing the students the text, I ask them to come up with cave and ice vocabulary. They don't know stalactite, stalagmite, spelunk but they sometimes know glacier, icicle.

Then I ask three questions. "Where are they?" "How high is he?" (pointing to the cat hanging from the ceiling), and "which picture was taken first?"

Then I read from the start of the article to the reference to massive icicles.

Most level 4 students can get where they are and how high he is. No students have discerned which picture was taken first just by my reading the text. Some students have not been able to figure it out after reading the text themselves.

Then I have them read the text and figure out the answer to the last question and ask me vocab. They ask about "belay" "hone" and "expedition." Maybe a couple others that I don't remember.

Then I ask three more questions, "How long has he been exploring glacier caves?" "Where has he been exploring?" and "How are these caves different from terrestrial caves?" (which necessitates definition of "terrestrial" (which helps them listen for the word to answer the question when I read)) and then I read the last column-ish.

Then they read / answer the questions and ask about "torture" and something else vocab-wise.

(*) I forgot to tell Miho and Makoto how to remember the names stalactite and stalagmite.
