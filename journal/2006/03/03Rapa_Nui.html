8:36pm CST Friday 03 March 2006

Rapa Nui was really nice. I wouldn't call it my *favorite* port, necessarily..

But

It was really really really really pretty.

The water is startlingly blue, and quite clear. It has a small sandy beach with gentle waves. It has a lot of rocky dangerous "beaches" with huge waves.

It's got Moai. Lots and lots of Moai.

They're just like in the pictures, except real. And now I can say I've been there. Easter Island. Me. Francois. PB. We did it!

- - - -

Oh, and in that crystal clear water beach, my underwater camera wasn't working, so I was shaking it and all sorts of stuff, tried to take another picture of Francois, and he wasn't there.

Oh shit.

I looked underwater repeatedly, but didn't find him. I told Carl. I didn't tell Phil because I was embarassed to say I cared about Francois that much, plus Phil was having fun pretty far out in the water.

Petra and Lisa happened by, and I told them to keep an eye out for Francois.

They were like, "what?? He's gone??"

Carl was all, "hell yeah, Francois saw found himself in the most beautiful beach in the world and said, 'that's it; I'm stayin' here!'"

I looked and looked, grew weary of holding my breath repeatedly, submerging and searching, all the while treading water, so I took a break, floating on my back, having given up and releasing Francois mentally.

I felt sad about the video team; how was I going to explain to them / recover a movie after my blunder?

I headed back to shore, continuing to look...

Found him.

"I found him!!"

Wow. Really happy.

He was flat face down on the sand, not moving; I must have drifted away from him.

- - - -

There were some awesome waves on some rocks. I had to go out and see them. I'm really thankful for the opportunity.

- - - -

There were rumors that four of the GET Ts had posed nakedly on top of the "mountain" at the Moai quarry.

- - - -

I ran around barefoot all day, and ended up with three splinters, a couple of gashes (that did not go deep enough to bleed), and 2 nicks that did bleed. Well worth a day a foot freedom.
