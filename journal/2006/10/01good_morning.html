1:23pm JST Monday 2 October 2006 (day 13340)

In Omote Sando, while waiting for the train, a girl two seats from me fell asleep. Our train came and everyone else got up except her. Following Japan's inability to communicate with strangers, I just bumped her arm a bit and made brief eye contact before walking to the train.

I could have easily started a conversation; she was quite thankful for having been woken up; she would have been screwed to miss last train (though this was only the second to last train), through which she easily could have snoozed. But, I didn't. I didn't think of the first thing to say which could have been おはよございます or 大丈夫？

She made eye contact with me and smiled thanks, and then smilingly thanked me again when she got off at her station (駒沢大学). I seem to have forgotten to be a bit outgoing as prescribed by my epiphany in Korea.

Hmmmm.
