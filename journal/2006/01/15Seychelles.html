7:15pm SCT Sunday 15 January 2006

Seychelles is an archipelago of 115 islands basically east of Madagascar. We stopped on Mahe at Port Victoria for a total of like 11 hours, giving us about 8.5 hours to explore. Therefore, I did not allow any time in an internet cafe. (( Mombassa is an overnight port, and only three days away, so I'll wait. Although I will be basically busy during our stay, I assume I'll be able to get to a cafe. ))

Today we walked to the main part of town, but most things were closed cause it's a Sunday. Took a taxi to "the beach," "which beach?" "we don't know," and ended up where everyone else ended up: Buffalo, er, make that "Beau Vallon" I believe. We were dropped off just several meters from the beach and even fewer meters from a dive shop, so we immediately got hooked up with snorkling and scuba diving utensils. $10 to snorkel (with $100 deposit) and $50 to dive. Glynis (sp) is quite friendly and immediately learned my name, which made me smile inside.

I met Paul, an experienced diver who lives in the area, and he gave me a few tips and stories as we waited for everyone to get their shiznit together.

14 divers, 3 dive masters went out on our little adventure to Group Point (I think) and dove in two groups of 6 plus a dive master, and 2 in buddy system, then 1 person to move the boat to our pickup location.

I dove alone-ish, mostly tagging along well behind our group. Didn't keep up cause I was caught up in taking pictures of Francois with fish. I had a bit of trouble going down, though we used the anchor rope. I got down to perhaps 15 meters. Used 150 bars of air in about 40 minutes.

Found myself floating up near the end of the dive, though I had released all the air from my vest (and hadn't dropped my weights, or anything) ... My theory is that since I only had 50 bar left in my tank, it was super floatier than it had been.

I had some sinus blockage problems near the end, which turned out to be loads of blood from my sinuses. Once I was back on the boat, I spat out gobs of blood snorted out the back of my sinus. Nice.

- - - -

Found Carla, Laura and Lisa at the end of their meal and I cleaned up the remains of Laura's lunch. Hung out on the beach watching some beach boys do gymnastics. They seemed obviously self-taught.

- - - -

Once we had about 1.25 hours until boarding limit, Nick, Phil and I began heading back. Glynnis (sp) drew a flawless map for us. I walked barefoot back to the port (3 kilometers); Nick and Phil paired up with GET T girls and hitched rides.

On my way back, I met Freda, who gave me fried bread fruit slices; also met Rie who gave me some black currant drink, and Rie's friend who gave me some cookies. I gave them some of the bread fruit.
