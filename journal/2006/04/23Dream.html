I had just joined a prison camp, where work was shared between everyone, but none of the rules were explained to me. The camp was very eco-conscious, and the first book I had to read was called _Green Sleeves_, written by a man who pioneered an eco-friendly way of doing laundry.

They said it was a short read, but the book, once I found it on the shelf, was huge. Like the size of a newspaper, and 2 inches thick.

I hadn't been made to do anything, like put on a prison uniform; all could be done at my own pace, but nothing was enforced, and in fact no one was really paying attention to me at all.
