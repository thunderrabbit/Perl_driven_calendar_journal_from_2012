10:52am JST Saturday 8 July 2006

I have been needing a calendar, but there seem to be none left in any of the stores; the annual cycle starts in May, so I should have been in the market while TJ Bike and I were galavanting around Japan.

I looked at some little notepads that I could use to make a DIM (Do It Myself) calendar, and selected one for 95 yen including tax. I like that it's all paper (save for spring binding) and looks like it may even be recycled paper.

I selected the pad, picked up some chocolate and ice cream and headed to the counter. I suddenly wondered if I could left hand the notepad in plain sight (also holding Hitomi's computer) while my right hand paid for the ice cream and chocolate.

Answer: yes.

Except for my troubled conscious, that was easier than easy.

On the way back, I thought about the US Government and lots of big business practices, plus the cultural norm of lifting things from the workplace. Decided this was small peanuts.
