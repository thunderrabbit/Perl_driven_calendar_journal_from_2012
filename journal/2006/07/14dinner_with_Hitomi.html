11:31pm JST Friday 14 July 2006

To ビックカメラ in 渋谷, but they didn't have the drive adapter. Headed to 新宿のヨドバシカメラ, and found just what we needed.

Wandered a little bit until we found an Italian restaurant where we enjoyed avacado/cheese/chicken goop in avacado shell, and thin (super thin, like corn chips) thin cheese pizza. I didn't really care for the salad (too vinegary) nor the smoking (too smokey) so we ducked out quickly, but not before I used the 1/6th crispy pizza slice as a new nose (props to Supa-funny Shawn on 49th for that idea) and nearly licked the avacado skin clean.

Back to the part of Shinjuku that I know in search of ice cream, but by that point the shops were closed or fast-closing as 10pm rolled by. As in, we walked out of the mall area as the guards were locking the doors.

Found a soft cream (soft serve ice cream) shop down the steps near GAP and ... it was okay, but not particularly vanillafied. McDonald's 100 yen shake would have been better.

仁美 suggested we wander around, so I led her to where I thought I had stayed my first night in Japan: the Sun Route Hotel in Shinjuku. Instead of the hotel, a construction project filled the lot.

"The hotel is gone???" Not likely that it wasn't getting enough business, being in such a fantastically crowded and wealthy city...

I told Hitomi why I had dragged her that direction, and she promptly pointed to the sign describing the construction.

Ah. ホテルソンロウト (Hotel Sun Route), being rebuilt on this site.

Very glad to know my sense of direction had led me well.

- - - -

Told her the story of losing my keys on my first day alone in the city, and buying 10 tickets (hoping for a discount) when I could only use 1, and how frightfully confusing Shinjuku is to a newbie, especially an illiterate one.

But now.. man I got this Tokyo shit figured out (*)... I even bought Hitomi her first Suica (which translates to water melon, but is the brand name for a cash card that works on JR lines).

(*) not figured out

Took the train to Shibuya where I hopped off, and she stayed on (til Shinagawa, then Kawasaki, then home).

- - - -

Walking through Shibuya when the creatures of the night have fully taken over, I felt both なつかし and a sense that this will be one of my last times to see it.

Am I heading home soon?
