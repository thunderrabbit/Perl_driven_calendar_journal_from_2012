5:44pm AST Tuesday 21 June 2005

Been walking alone around Athens (I think), which seems pretty darn close to Piraeus, where TSS The Topaz floats waiting for midnight to arrive.

I saw Ai and some other girls on some rocks where I had been barefootedly rock-hopping. We got pictures taken together.

I wore no shoes while walking around Acropolis, and therefore was disallowed entry to one of the sites. I neither felt like arguing nor relenting when the woman said I would have to wear shoes before I could enter. The building cannot be described as "inside" nor sterile nor anything else I can imagine would require shoes. I simply put away my wallet and left.

I don't feel like putting on shoes.

Afterward, a double scoop of ice cream (from the shop next to which I had previously seen a turtle) sedated my grumpy brain while I wrote a few post cards. I headed back to the train station where I had begun my walking around the area and bought some snacks for tonight's After Greece Chow Party: cheese and crackers, cherries and peaches.

Fell asleep on the train back to Piraeus station and noted how the ticketing system require a great deal of honesty among the passengers, who can easily bypass or ignore the gates.

On the way back to the docks, I felt tempted a few times to buy a porn magazine. Visible at every kiosk along the street, they proudly flashed boobs for all to see. I resisted the urge to buy.

However, I did not resist buying a Vanilla Cream Frappacino from Starbucks, and I enjoyed slurping it when I met Chiko along the way back, when she asked for some assistance in crossing the street. The lack of crosswalks seems to have thrown a spin on her good sensibilities. We crossed the street without a crosswalk and without incident.

The vanilla cream on top of the Frappacino tasted thick and delicious.

The dock entry area appears to have closed or suffered a blackout, for darkness trumps lighting, and the vendors' wares no longer beckon outside the shops. I felt thankful to purchase one more stamp from the closing shop.

I saw two guys ticketing parked cars. One would write the ticket, and the other would remove the license plates. I imagine this method is quite cheap for the city and quite effective!
