10:22pm JST Monday 07 February 2005

I plan to expand this to a full blown couple of entries, but in case I don't, I'll at least have something.

We came back to Sapporo and walked around the 11 blocks featuring snow things. I got a photo with Miss Hong Kong 2004. Sweet. She was whisked away before I could get into any more trouble.

As we were nearly back to our hotel, I saw a sign indicating an observatory 450 meters away. Kick ass. Thinking I could pick something up for Miss Jackie Purdy, we headed that direction. But couldn't find anything like an observatory.

We did, however, find lots of snow, deep enough to have covered a set of bicycles that had been parked before it started.

I discovered I had the opportunity to try and fulfill one of my lifelong projects: dig a tunnel through the snow.

Things I figured out before starting:

  1. Don't stand (and therefore pack down) the snow where you want to dig.

Things I figured out after starting:

  1. Waterproof gloves, jacket, pants, boots are good for this type of venture.

  2. Start digging away from the beaten path so people won't fall into the hole (*)

  3. Have warm food available for chowing; this is not easy work.

  4. Once

(*) no one slipped into the hole, but I didn't feel comfortable leaving the tunnel until morning; I smushed it into a not so deep hole before leaving.
