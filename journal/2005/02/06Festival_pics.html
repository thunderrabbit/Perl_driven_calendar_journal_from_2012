10:25am JST Saturday 02 April 2005

I'm finally putting together pics from the festival.

Most of what I've got were taken in Sapporo. Most were all along the street that has the TV tower on it.

First, all the big ones. I don't know much/anything about these: who designed them who made them who likes them who knows what? I don't know. But they were pretty neat to see.

This one was close to the TV tower, and close to where I met Miss Hong Kong 2004.

We saw some musicians on stage here. Flute and violin maybe.

Here the magic was kinda taken away: I can clearly see the lines where layers of ice were put together. Seems like there could be a way to blend these more seemlessly. Like allowing water to freeze between the chunks. Ah well.

A bunch of kids all in line waiting to slide down this hill reminded me of sledding down hills with Fred back in the day. I couldn't imagine having to wait in line for such a short little hill as this one was. But still I guess it could be cool to little kids.

This one was my second favorite. It had neat 3D curves and shapes not present in the others.

This one was my favorite. I liked how it wasn't a stage. It didn't have any way to generate money. No indication of who made it or who would profit. Just art. A beautiful rendition that reminds me of the architecture around the Imperial Palace in Tokyo. Except a lot smaller. And made of snow.

Here is the back.
