11:10am IST Wednesday 12 October 2005

The terrain from Qalandia to Nablus remins me a lot of West Texas.. rolling hills of arid rockiness, with consistently sparse vegetation. Trees like brave shrubs grow in clumps where water visits; the hills have an almost terraced appearance, as if they have been cut into architecture student models of mountains, made from stacks of cardboard.

Like an impressionist painting, little speckles of vegetation give a 50% coverage over these hills.

Our two lane asphalt highway cuts through like a long scribbled scar across this desert plain, that features occasional warts of buildings on its face.

Just saw a grove of young olive trees, each protected by a painted oil drum barrel that will each have to be cut off when the trees are old enough to fend for themselves.

Max, Anna and I are arranged right to left in the taxi's back seat; three guys in the mid seat and one in the passengers seat, plus the driver.
