8:02am JST Friday 15 April 2005

I think I went to sleep around 10pm. During the night, between probably 12 and 2 am, I heard a strange sing-song noise that I recognized as a girl animatedly talking. I guessed she was a loud American yammering with her quieter friend. Then I recognized some Japanese words, and I recognized she was animatedly upsetly crying talking on her cell phone. Good vibes sent her way.

This morning I woke up around 5:45 am, feeling basically refreshed and happy, though I hadn't particularly slept for two continuous ~4 hour stretches like normal. I did a lot of flipping around since I was sleeping on a slight incline, and the ground wasn't as soft as my futon.

I packed up my gear, gave a blessing to the church for offering the space, and to the trees for protecting me, and to the weather for staying clear and dew free. Made sure I had all my stuff and my trash and headed out.

Down the hills like ZZZZooooOOOOooooOOOOoooooOOOomomoommmmmmmmmm flying around corners all zoooooomoooOOOoooOOooomm sailing down the straight parts all ZZZozzozozoooooaooaoaooozoozozozooooommmmm tears pouring out of my eyes from the crispy wind, then slow slow just keep swimming just keep swimming as I went up that shortish stretch of hill, at the top of which I got some pictures. Then down the rest ZzzzzzozozozozoooooooooOOOOooOOOOOoommmmm reliving in reverse all the places I had toiled so hard to climb. Down past all the places I had planned to stop ZzzzzzozozoOOOOOOOOOoooOoOom past the mysterious steps that climbed down to the highway zZZZZzzzzzoooooOOOoooooOOOOoomm past the ZZzzzozzzzoooooooooOOOOOooOOooOoooooooMMMMM past the statues that had blessed my ascent yesterday ZzzzoooooOOOooommmm past the well manicured flowerbed with ハコネ written in blossoming flowers Zzoooo eerrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrtt!! (stopping noise). A truck was fully stopped in the middle of the lane. His wheels seemed to be smoking; I'm sure it was his brakes. Little bit too much zooom. I waited for a clear moment in traffic then zipped around him, blessing his journey and my journey and TJ Bike's brakes. Then zzzzozoozoooooooooOOOoooooooooooOOoooooommmm all the way to the bottom of the hill where I arrived back at that station at the end of Odakyu line and looked for my three rickshaw buds.

Of course they were not there; it was probably only 7am, and there weren't many tourists out.

Oh, that reminds me: during my AM descent, most traffic was going UP the hill; in to Gora for the day. I had a pretty clear shot all the way down save for that truck. Oh, and a wide crane thing going uphill, taking up more than half the road.

Other than that, it's been pretty straightforward. Just heading back along Route 1 国道１ (what's the pronunciation for these kanji?)

I'm stopped in 小磯 （おいそ）at Denny's using their electricty to write this entry.
