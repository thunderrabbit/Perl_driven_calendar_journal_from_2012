We are at her favorite bookstore, The Strand, featuring 18 miles of books. We have specified that we'll meet back at the front at exactly 3:40pm. She and I both love books slash bookstores, so we could be here for a while, but that could get expensive (and heavy).

It's so weird to see all these books in English! Man; there is so much to read!

12:05am

I am in a cab heading back to the boat. Just hung out with Paige and her peeps at Bowery Bar, where we played Mafia (I narrated the games) twice. The first game was short cause I hadn't done a good job of describing the rules:

I handed out shuffled pieces of my business card to specify who would be commissioner, mafia, townspeople. Everyone saw their little piece and I specified, "okay, don't show anyone your card. whoever has my first name, 'Rob' is the mafia. Whoever has 'Nugen' is the commissioner. Everyone else is a townsperson." Everyone understood, and the first night came. I was like, "okay, everyone put your heads down. Mafia person, wake up and touch one person on the head." I had my head down, too, because we didn't really have enough people to play otherwise. "Okay, everyone wake up, except the person who was touched: you have died," and then for effect, "Oh No!!" So one guy woke up all dramatically dead and then I was like, "aight guys; who do you think did it?"

And one guy goes, "I did it," like, well *that* was an easy question!

I was like oops and explained the rules more carefully the next time.

Oh my gosh, *AND* I met a man named Earth who (looks a lot like my cousin Peter, and is superfluent in Japanese, and has a birthday soon, AND) knows Silent Football!!!! I was describing the overview of the game, and he goes, "oh, you mean Silent Football?" and my heart lept like, WOW! "yeah! you know it??"

He said he learned it in college and showed me some of the moves he remembered. They were different than what we use in Southwest District, but similar moves to what I remember from some of the UU groups up in the northeast US..

We didn't actually play SF, but had a great time chattering and such. My first night out in New York City was a great success.

At one point I saw Earth's shirt was wrong side out, so I said, "hey Earth, why is your shirt inside out?"

He goes, "wha?" and looked and "hey, my shirt is inside out!"

So I turned my shirt inside out as well, just to be the first one in on this new fashion statement. Paige joked about turning her own shirt inside out, but it would have been a bit more revealing than our button shirts over T-shirts.
