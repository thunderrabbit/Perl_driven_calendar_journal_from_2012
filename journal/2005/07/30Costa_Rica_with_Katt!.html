9:54am CST Saturday 30 July 2005

Katt rescued me from my room (bless her), and after walking for a while on the beach, where I 'helped' the guys who were cleaning the beach for what seems like preparation for a big event tonight, we walked around this little peninsula. We walked past what might be considered the shady area of town, because there was shade, and people with rather sharp knives were gutting fish among junk cars and corrugated metal sheds. On the water, boats floated next to non-flotatious boats (or at least not very modern boats) and flamingoes chilled in various cliques.

Near the end of the peninsula, we found a park that was just about completely overrun with plants. It featured a swimming pool, but the water was only about 18 inches deep, and quite dark. I enjoyed seeing trees growing completely beyong the tires that had protected them when they were little.

I would have found a way into the pool, but Katt was anti-interested in that. She left the park after not too long.

We continued walking, and at the end of the peninsula, we spent several minutes trying to get a photo of Francois flying toward the camera as I stood in the water. I wanted my arms to be at my sides so it wouldn't be apparent he had been thrown. This one is the best we got.

It was after this game that we met a motorcycle rider. He had stopped, possibly wondering wtf we were doing with this stuffed rabbit. I felt assured he wouldn't think I was a pansy with a bunny so much as a weird guy with a stuffed rabbit, cause I certainly wasn't taking pains to treat Francois as if he were delicate. He landed all over the rocks and asphalt and we laughed each time as I ran up the rocks (barefootedly, of course) to recover him if he wasn't within Katt's easy reach.

Afterward, I went up to talk to him. He's been riding around for some months and will sell it soon and go to Chile and live for a while. I wondered how he financed his adventure and he goes, "Ah.. I have income."

So Katt and I were wondering how he has money. She thinks network marketing, and I think something he built with his own blood sweat and tears, which could include network marketing.

11am CST Saturday 30 July 2005

Yo y Katorin estan en el taxi. Nosotros vamos al mono y jungla (el junglo?) y miemos un rio con muchos arbores. Que bueno!

12:05pm CST Saturday 30 July 2005

Just saw crocodiles under the bridge. I took a video of them. I wanted to have a thin long rope so I could lower Francois down to their level and see how big they were compared to him. They were basking by a river over which our highway passed. Lots of people were stopped in the area to scope out the crocs. Lots of traffic rumbled by, making for a relatively dangerous situation: there was no real guard between people on the narrow "sidewalk" (which basically amounted to a wide curb) and the highway traffic, that often included large 18-wheelers.

1:45pm CST Saturday 30 July 2005

A dog that could be named Rex, Katt next to a pretty big tree, me on the suspension brige.

Katt and I walked through this cloud forest area (called Rainforest Aerial Trams) and loved watching the leaf cutter ants. She created obstacles for them by putting a big leaf in their path. She wanted to know how long it would take for them to find their way across it. Basically they didn't before she got bored of waiting and removed the leaf.

I was amazed at the sheer length of their trails. They would travel for several meters each direction before disappearing into the jungle. We kept seeing them cross our trail and I wondered how many different colonies they represented. Was it the same ant-highway crossing our trail multiple times? And why can't these guys find leaves closer to their home? Surely they don't have to walk 20 meters in the jungle to find a leaf!

Hilariously, Katt complained that this is a "rainforest" but wan't raining, and it better rain cause she brought her umbrella.

Soon thereafter, it began to rain, and she was all happy with her umbrella, plus, the trees did an excellent job of dispersing the rain. Then it came time to go back across the small suspension bridge to go back across the river.

Her umbrella wouldn't fit between the lines! She had to fold it up to walk across, and she got soaked. Aww poooor Katt!

My watch also go soaked, and it now shows blank-o'clock.

We ate at a cafe, and Katt got alligator burger.
