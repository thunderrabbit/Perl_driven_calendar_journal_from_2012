Amazing.

Donald is a good natured missionary who believes wholeheartedly that the King James Bible is the authoritative word of God.

He says slavery was not condoned in the bible. "It refers to bondmen, who were obligated to repay a debt. The simple word 'bond' explains it. There was an agreement; a bond. It's not slavery."

Ben looked up bondman. "bondman, noun: a male bound to serve without wages. a male slave."

"I don't trust people who write dictionaries."

Donald says continental drift is a lie; there is no way continents can move around. "Take an egg and crack its shell. Can the pieces slide around? No; they are all bumping into one another."

Sarah explained that the plates crash into one another to create mountains, and sometime go under one another causing earthquakes.

"If it's been happening for millions of years, as you say, why aren't the mountains higher? It's just a ridiculous proposal to ignore what they can't explain."

Donald disproves the theory of evolution by pointing out that we can't explain where all the matter came from in the universe. "Before the Big Bang, was there matter, or wasn't there? It's a simple question."

Ben goes, "by definition, if there is a point at which the laws of physics break down, we can't know what happened beyond that point." He continues, "but that has nothing to do with the origin of species; there were billions of years between the Big Bang and any life."

"The Origin of Species means the Origin of Matter. It's as simple as that."

A different topic, that was more complicated than I can realistically capture, went something like this:

Ben: "settlements are illegal."

Donald: "no they're not."

Ben: "yes they are; under the 4th Geneva Convention."

Donald: "Geneva Convention? What's that? When was it written?"

Ben: "Right after World War II."

Donald: "settling happens all over the world. Take Texas for example; the Americans invaded Mexico to take over the Mexican land."

Ben: "Ignoring that happened before the Geneva Convention was written, because an international law is broken by one country doesn't make it right for any country to break the law."

Donald: "That law doesn't exist."

Ben: "Yes it does."

Donald: "It's the spoils of war. Take Kuwait for example. Iraq attacked them without cause, the bastards, and Kuwait was able to hold them back. Had Kuwait gone ahead and taken the land, more power to them."

Ben: "I'm not talking about Kuwait or Texas. I'm simply saying that settlements are illegal under the 4th Geneva Convention."

Donald: "Whoever wrote that was out of their minds."
