As of 3.14 weeks ago, my job has been to clean the bathroom and take out the garbage. (Michael's job has been to vaccuum the floor and take out the recycling.)

Today, as each Friday morning since then, I've done a Rob-version of cleaning the bathroom, which is basically to clear everything out, scrub everything down, dry everything off, put everything back. All while naked, of course.

For cleaning purposes, there are two good thing about Japanese bathrooms:

  1. They are small.

  2. (and this is the big one) They are waterproof.

Ha ha; sounds funny, I know. But, in the US, I could not spray water all over the floor: it would soak into the carpet in the adjacent room. And, there was no drain.

In all the bathrooms I've seen in Japan (and I've seen several), the floor is smooth solid plastic or tile, and it's got a barricade at the door to keep any water from escaping the wrong way. And it has a drain to let the water escape the desired direction: down the tubes.

The shower head in our bathroom is portable; it's got a hose connecting to the faucets, so I can spray down all the walls and the floor and everything. I do this happily. Happily violating an old household rule: Boys, don't splash water out of the tub!

Then, I take my own shower, dry off, dry off the bathroom mirror, walls, toilet, and floor. Then toss the towel into the washing machine.

Presto: clean bathroom, happy Rob
