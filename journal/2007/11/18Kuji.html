I'm writing now about ultimate yesterday.

Best parts of the day:

  1. on the way to the field, Ami and I passed a little (!) lot of persimon trees. The farmer was there and he gave us about 10, which I loaded into my pockets and headed to the field.

  2. Genny with a G brought cookies!! Chocolate chip with dried raspberries, I think! They were delicious, I know! Too bad for Jason C; he got none cause I snarfed like 100. They were soooo good. When she talked about starting a cookie sales company, I told her I want a standing order of five dozen per week. She asked "is that 12 or a baker's dozen?" I was like, "a Genny dozen." She goes, "okay, three."

  3. Rob threw a really long pass that I *bolted* over to catch and barely maintained my balance as it slammed into my thenar eminence (*) as I caught it. Jason C and Wendi both said it was a good catch; Jason doesn't give out compliments very often so my sore hand felt vindicated.

  4. Ami played! She ran around on the field for some points, and seemed as interested and confused as a new player should be. Great job!

  5. the sky was beautiful and clear. I think I noticed an undular bore wave as we were walking back.

(*) five minutes ago, I did not know the term "thenar eminence"

Thing that were not as pleasant as the good things:

  1. It was so cold!

  2. I glonked the fuck out of my knee in the endzone when I fell while squirming around Geoff for an attempted point in the endzone. I almost caught it, but then was pretty much in pain and not able to run well. Some of the others encouraged me off the field to take care of it.

I got another cookie and watched Ami play as I sat on the water faucet thing letting a little stream of cold water run on my now purple spotted knee.

After about five minutes of water dribblage, I figured it had been long enough so joined the group as they were packing up to leave.

I enjoyed holding Ami's hand on the way back.

Things are chicken soup with us.
