Thanks to Jesse's camera, I was able to get some pics of Marble Track 2. I'm working on another zig zag for big marbles that will basically mirror the one that has been installed for a while. I've been stuck in the detail of getting the switch to work. It's been far more finicky than I'd like, but it seems that it's come to a point that will work. There is a stabilizer on each end of its rotation, and, most importantly, the bottom actually switch part seems to work.

If I can knock out the rest of the switch soon (need to make one more gate (on the top) to toggle the switch and allow the ball to go through, then there will just be one more super-difficult part to do.

I've got to gently lower the large marbles about 3 or 4cm and 3 or 4cm horizontally onto a track with basically no side rails, and no room to build any. So I've got a scoop-thing under construction that will rotate down (and a counterweight on back to make it "slow" (I wonder how slow it can realistically go)), release the big marble on its track, and then lift back out of the way so the small marbles can go by on the adjacent track.

I'm thinking of what to use as the counterweight. Perhaps I can lift an entire section of track, and allow small marbles to be triggered into action from a stopping point, perhaps a couple at a time, that will go down different tracks.

Students' most common question is on the lines of "do you have some sort of blueprints for this?" and most of them cannot fathom creating something 'freestyle' like this. I basically say there is no blueprint as in a master plan, but I do have these varied short term visions of what it could do, but they're quite vague, and change on a whim.

I just "decide where I want the marble to go, and then figure out how to make it go there." Bonus points for cramming tracks in small places, and for gentle descents.
