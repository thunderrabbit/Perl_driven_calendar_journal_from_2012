I did not bring my computer to my art presentation, but I brought some blank paperboard things (I forgot the name in Japanese, but they're often used as signature cards to give as thank youus.) On the train I drew four different pieces, just for an extra boost of things to show at the show.

In attendance: Rimi (organizer), Akiko (my friend from English Village), Mayumi (Akiko's friend from their class on art, Yuko and Yuko, friends of both Akiko and Rimi. One goes by Yu-chan and the other by Yuko, though I kept forgetting which was who so I just called them both Yuko.

I spoke sorta freestyle about the Q "what is art?" and sorta asked some Qs of them and I'm not sure what else, but then had everyone draw on a blank paperboard (I had three big ones and three little ones available so I had pairs じゃんけん to see who got which). Those pieces became exquisite corpses (as far as I understand) and most of them (except the one I started) turned out reasonably interesting, I think.

At the end of the presentation, I asked everyone to choose a piece as a gift, and a color with which I signed the back and gave them. Yuko chose "Anna's Spirals," Yuko chose "Arthor McKinneson," Akiko chose "Spaghetti Spoon," Mayumi chose "No one on 3 Alien Highway," and Rimi chose "Three Ways In," I believe.
