The last one was a source of great entertainment for us, as they tried to figure out what the densely colored portion of the drawing signified. Basically I would not tell them except, "when you know, you'll know." and soon enough, Yuko got it, and then Akiko got it after that, but she kept saying, "well, maybe," so I was like, "no you didn't get it," and then she "gained confidence," so I was like, "aight," and called the piece 二人のhomework, a mangling of Japanese ("2 people") and English ("homework") and a の in between which indicates ownership.

They made joke plans to call each other to figure out the answer and teams of those in the know and those who will go to their graves with the question on top of their mind.

On the way from the coffee shop to the station, I changed the name of 二人のhomework to 三人の homework, because it turns out Akiko did *not* figure it out.
