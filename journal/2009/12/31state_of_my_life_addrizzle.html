The long versions are あけましておめでとございます and ことしもよろしくおねがいします (there is Kanji for these (at least the second one), but I've spelled them all out to point out just how short each one is (each character ends up being a syllable of the phrases, so in the first case from 14 to 4 syllables, and the second from 15 to 4 syllables.

    The point being they're really short. A bit shorter even than just saying H-Y in English to mean Happy New Year!
