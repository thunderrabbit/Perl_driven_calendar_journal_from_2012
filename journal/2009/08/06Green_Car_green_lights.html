I just found one more awesome thing about Japanese train system.

The JR system uses "Suica" debit cards on which people put money and then pay for trains. Right now I'm in the Green Car of the Shonan Shinjuku Line which requires one to pay a machine outside the train for the Green Car seat. Only reason to pay outside is so that we have a big touch screen to tell our destination so it knows how much to charge the card.

On the train, we sit down and touch the card to a little red light on the ceiling which determines we have paid and turns green. Then the train conductor can come through and make sure everyone is sitting under a green light, and appropriately chastise those who are not.

Though that's all cool, I've seen it all before. The cool thing I just now saw was a guy changing seats! I was like, yo! he's paid, but is now sitting under a red light and then he touched his card to turn his light green and the light over his old seat turned red. Awesome.
